The Fundamentals of Chess

The Chessboard

A chessboard consists of 64 squares, arranged in an 8×8 grid with alternating light and dark colors. Each square is identified by a unique coordinate—a letter (a-h) and a number (1-8).

Piece Movement

  • King - Moves one square in any direction
  • Queen - Moves any number of squares diagonally, horizontally, or vertically
  • Rook - Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically
  • Bishop - Moves any number of squares diagonally
  • Knight - Moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction and one square perpendicular
  • Pawn - Moves forward one square, but captures diagonally

Basic Rules

When a king is under attack, it's in "check." The player must get out of check on their next move. If there's no way to escape check, it's "checkmate" and the game is over.

Chess Openings

Ruy Lopez

One of the oldest and most classic of all openings. It is named after a Spanish bishop who wrote one of the first books on chess.

Key moves: 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bb5

Sicilian Defense

The most popular and best-scoring response to White's first move 1.e4. Known for creating asymmetrical positions.

Key moves: 1.e4 c5

Queen's Gambit

One of the oldest openings in chess, beginning with the moves 1.d4 d5 2.c4. It's a classic weapon at all levels of play.

Key moves: 1.d4 d5 2.c4
